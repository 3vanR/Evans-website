The Devil in Pop Culture

Embodiment, Abjection, and Monstrosity

Introduction

This exhibition explores how the devil is portrayed in modern popular culture, focusing on themes of embodiment, abjection, and monstrosity. Drawing from religious symbolism and narratives, these portrayals in films, music, art, and fashion reveal how societal fears and taboos are projected onto this figure of evil. By blending historical and contemporary perspectives, the exhibition investigates the evolving role of the devil in shaping cultural anxieties and norms.

Religious traditions have long depicted the devil as a symbol of transgression and moral corruption. Today, popular culture reimagines this figure to challenge traditional notions of good and evil. For example, Lil Nas X's "Montero" reclaims satanic imagery to confront societal biases around sexuality, while films like The Exorcist amplify the devil's terror to explore moral dilemmas. These reinterpretations highlight how the devil serves as a lens to examine societal issues, such as identity, power, and rebellion.
